Eye tracking

Eye tracking in user research has gained a lot of attention lately, and much due to the fact that the technology has become more accessible. But far too many are using it to gather large chunks of data, without proper research questions.

In this talk I will talk about how to use eye tracking in user research and how it can bring you extra insights â€“ but also, when not to use it. I will talk about why using it in the wrong way could actually give misleading insights.

The presentation will also include my personal experience with using eye tracking as a lone UX designer in a small, agile product development team. I will talk about bringing insights to your team and how to involve them in design decisions.
